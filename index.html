Flight Data Visualisation

Introduction

This project presents a visualisation of global flight data. Through an exploration of flight routes, ticket prices, and flight disruptions, we aim to uncover insights into the factors influencing air travel. Our goal is to provide a clear understanding of the patterns and anomalies in the aviation industry, offering a comprehensive overview that goes beyond surface-level observations. These visualisations allow us to better understand the interplay between travel demand, pricing strategies, and operational challenges faced by airlines.

Flight Path Network Diagram

The visualisation on the left illustrates global flight routes, connecting cities across continents. Here, we observe key hubs with the highest number of flights, predominantly located in North America and Europe. This suggests that these regions play a central role in the global air travel network. Each line on the map signifies a route, and by examining these connections, we can identify how international travel links cities and how geographic location impacts air traffic density.

Flight Duration vs Average Ticket Price Scatterplot

This scatterplot explores the relationship between flight duration and average ticket price. Interestingly, we find that not all longer flights are more expensive. Some short-duration flights show unexpectedly high prices, likely due to factors such as demand, flight frequency, or limited competition. This visualisation helps to identify trends in airline pricing strategies, revealing the complexity behind ticket pricing beyond just flight duration.

Flight Delays and Cancellations per Hour of Day

Flight delays and cancellations are common concerns for travelers. In this chart, we examine how disruptions in air travel vary by time of day. Peaks during late afternoon and early evening reveal when flight operations are more likely to encounter delays or cancellations, potentially due to the cumulative effect of earlier flights or increased congestion. Understanding these patterns is crucial for both passengers and airline operators to optimize flight schedules and mitigate disruptions.
